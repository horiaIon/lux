bazaart

A market for talent

Role - UX Designer
Year - 2022
Client - SIIT Scoala Informala de IT

OVERVIEW

Bazaart mobile app is a UX Case study project on which I have worked as part of my assignment during the UX Bootcamp at SIIT (Scoala informala de IT).

THE GOAL

The initial idea from which we started was : Hire an artist - an online platform where freelance artists could promote their work and sell their artistic services.

MY ROLE

During this project, I have worked both as part of a team and individually :research was a team effort followed by an individual design phase. I went through all the stages of the Design Thinking Process, from understanding users and defining their needs to creating deliverables such as wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes and then using the MVP created to do user testing.

THE PROBLEM

Firstly, we started by doing exploratory research in order to gather some ideas about this topic and help us define the problem, since this was a new product and we didn’t knew where in the market this app would fit. Based on this information we established a set of assumptions and hypothesis, what we know and what we know we don’t. This allowed us to elaborate a research plan: what problem are we trying to address and what questions we should ask.

QUESTIONS

+ How people are currently finding artists for their specific needs?

+ What pain points do they encounter when trying to find the right artist ?

+ How artists are currently finding clients and promoting their work portfolio?

+ What pain points do they have with the current process of finding clients?

+ What categories of creative services people are searching for most often?

+ What are the situations or projects for which people need creative services?

+ What are users expectations regarding this process?

RESEARCH

After we knew what we need to find out and we had defined our research goals, we dived into the actual research: competitor analysis, quantitative research (surveys) and qualitative research (interviews).

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Based on some exploratory research regarding our competition, I have selected for comparison one app from the Romanian market and two more internationally renowned apps in order to identify possible opportunities.

SURVEYS + INTERVIEWS

We each contributed to writing the survey and interview scripts and then conducted the actual interviews with users. During the interviews, we have came across some interesting findings regarding people’s habits when it comes to searching for creative services or clients for their services, and the problems they are facing in this process.

AFFINITY DIAGRAM

The next step was to break down all the data we gathered through a process of coding and recoding, organizing and structuring, to uncover meaning. Then using deductive codes, I grouped the pieces of information together in categories in order to highlight the main features of our app.

USER PERSONA

Based on the interviews, two users personas were created representative of each user group: the artist and the buyer, to further understand users needs, experiences, behaviors, and goals. The personas created summarized our user research data to help us shape the product design strategy.

DESIGN

After the research phase was completed, the next step in the process was creating user flows diagram to display the path a user could take when using this app. I had begun with the general user flow, which encompasses the main features of the app in order to have a general image of the product, and then proceed to detail one feature of the app. By considering each step within the app, I was able to enhance the user journey and ensure maximum smoothness. The feature I focused on was that of creating a new event and finding, adding and requesting quotes from multiple artists for that particular event.

WIREFRAMES

After structuring the user flow, I started sketching ideas on how the screen would look using pen and paper, at first, to create them faster and explore more ideas. Further, a low fidelity prototype was generated to help envision the screens better.

HI-FI MOCKUPS

I started the high fidelity prototyping by first working on a design system in order to maintain design consistency. The high fidelity mockup was created based on the previous exploration done with the help of the wireframes and explores the app from the perspective of the buyers account.

USABILITY TESTING

Using the prototype created with the high fidelity mockups, I have divided the flow into 3 main tasks for conducting usability testing with 3 participants. Seeing users interact with the prototype helped me identify areas that I could improve upon.

Create a new event -> set the details of your new event
Add artists -> browsing artists and adding them to your event
Manage booked artists -> checking quotes status and accepting/declining the offer received

TAKEAWAY

In summary, the key takeaway is that it is essential to approach every project with an open mind and to conduct meticulous research to identify the needs of the target audience, validating each hypothesis as you go along. By abandoning preconceived notions and prioritizing data-driven decisions, you can create products that are truly valuable and meet the needs of your users.
